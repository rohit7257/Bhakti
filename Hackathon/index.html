About Moksh Marg

Moksh Marg is a dedicated platform for pilgrimage travelers in India. We assist devotees in planning and booking their journeys to holy places like Char Dham, Jyotirlingas, Shakti Peethas, Pancha Bhoota Temples, and Sapta Puri. Our goal is to make spiritual travel seamless by providing ticket booking, bus reservations, and guided pilgrimage tours.
Hinduism: Four Paths (Yogas) to Moksha:-
1. Jnana Yoga (Path of Knowledge) – Attaining wisdom through self-inquiry and meditation.
2. Bhakti Yoga (Path of Devotion) – Surrendering to God with pure devotion.
3. Karma Yoga (Path of Selfless Action) – Performing actions without attachment to the results.
4. Raja Yoga (Path of Meditation) – Practicing meditation and self-discipline to attain enlightenment.

Ayodhya

Ayodhya is an ancient and sacred city in Uttar Pradesh, India. It is one of the seven most important pilgrimage sites (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism and is revered as the birthplace of Lord Rama, the seventh incarnation of Vishnu. It forms the Rama Janmabhoomi and is a major pilgrimage destination.

Mathura

Mathura, located in Uttar Pradesh, India, is one of the holiest cities in Hinduism. It is revered as the birthplace of Lord Krishna, the eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Along with Vrindavan, it forms the Krishna Janmabhoomi and is a major pilgrimage destination.

Places Associated with Moksh Marg:
Many sacred places in India are considered paths to Moksha, including:
Haridwar – A gateway for purification through the Ganges.
Ayodhya – Spiritual centers of Lord Rama.
Varanasi – The city where salvation is believed to be attained.
Mathura – Spiritual centers of Krishna.
Conclusion : Moksh Marg is not just a physical journey but a spiritual transformation. It requires self-discipline, devotion, and wisdom to break free from worldly attachments and unite with the divine.

Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major spiritual center in India. Located on the banks of the holy Ganges River in Uttar Pradesh, it is considered the city of Lord Shiva and one of the most sacred places for Hindus.

Haridwar

Haridwar, located in Uttarakhand, is one of the holiest cities in India. It is known as the "Gateway to the Gods" because it is the starting point of the Char Dham Yatra. Situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, it is one of the seven holiest Hindu pilgrimage sites.
